1A-i & 1A-ii — Login & National ID

Registration is a two-step process on first login only. After that, returning users go straight to the home screen after the OTP step.

  • Step 1 (1A-i): Phone number → free SMS OTP → verify. No password ever.
  • Step 2 (1A-ii): National ID number + full name. This is the one-time identity check that ties every parent to a real, verifiable person before any authorization can be issued.
  • Progress dots (1 of 2, 2 of 2) show the user exactly where they are — no surprise steps
  • ID card photo is optional — admin can still manually verify with just the ID number
  • Step 2 only appears on first login — not on every subsequent login
Why this matters: In As-Is Scenario 1, Faith's identity was never verified at any point in the process. This step ensures every parent in the system is a named, verifiable person before they can authorize anyone to collect a child.
Jakob's Law: OTP via SMS mirrors M-Pesa, eCitizen, and KCB flows — zero new learning required.

What the authorized picker sees when they arrive at the school gate. They show either the QR code or read out the 6-digit OTP to the teacher.

  • Works offline — QR/OTP pre-downloaded when auth was sent

4A — Admin Home Dashboard

The administrator's central command view, designed for a desktop or laptop browser. It gives the school admin a real-time snapshot of the entire closing day operation at a single glance — how many students have been released, how many authorizations are active, any pending Pickup Circle approvals, and a live feed of gate events.

  • 6-stat summary grid — Total enrolled, authorizations issued, students released, pending approvals, declined attempts, and emergency overrides all visible without scrolling
  • Pending Pickup Circle approvals — Admin can approve or reject new circle members directly from the dashboard with one click, replacing the current manual paper-based process
  • Live gate activity feed — Real-time list of every pickup event (authorized, declined, override) as they happen at the gate; replaces the unupdated handwritten register found in field observations
  • Emergency Override shortcut — Prominent button at the top right, since emergencies require the fastest possible access path
  • Web-based, desktop-first — Administrators in Kenyan public schools work from desktops or laptops; this screen is not mobile-optimized unlike the parent and teacher interfaces
Addresses: The 72% of administrators who reported having no digital record of past pickup events (questionnaire finding, Chapter 2). Every event shown here is automatically timestamped and stored in D4: Audit Logs.

4B — Emergency Override

A dedicated admin-only screen that handles the most critical and sensitive scenario in SafePass — releasing a student to a person who is not on the pre-approved Pickup Circle due to an urgent, unforeseen situation. This directly replaces the dangerous verbal phone authorizations described in As-Is Scenario 2 (Kevin Kamau's medical emergency).

  • Student search — Admin finds the student quickly by name or admission number; student details are confirmed before proceeding to prevent errors under pressure
  • Emergency picker details — Admin enters the emergency picker's National ID, phone number, and relationship to the student — all logged permanently in the audit trail
  • Mandatory reason field — Admin must state the reason for the override in writing. This creates the accountability and legal record that was completely absent in the old verbal phone authorization system where principals had "no way to record or document" their decision
  • 30-minute token validity — Emergency tokens are short-lived by design; the narrow time window limits misuse while remaining practical for the real situation
  • Automatic parent notification — Parent receives an SMS the moment the override is issued, ensuring full transparency even if they were the ones who triggered the request
  • Principal fallback clause — If the parent cannot be reached for notification, the system flags this and requires a physical principal signature as a backup accountability measure
Addresses UC-04: The emergency override use case — covering Mzee Peter's scenario where an unlisted grandfather needed to collect an injured Kevin Kamau but was turned away at the gate because no digital mechanism existed to authorize him in real time.

4C — Audit Logs & Reports

The full historical record of every pickup event that has ever passed through SafePass — searchable, filterable, and exportable. This screen directly addresses the most alarming finding from the administrator interviews: "If something happened after a student left with someone, we have no record of who authorised it or when." That gap is permanently closed here.

  • Timestamped log table — Every row records the exact time, student name, picker identity, authorizing parent, event type, outcome (Authorized / Declined / Override), and token type (QR or OTP). The full authorization chain is visible for any disputed entry
  • Filter bar — Admin can filter by event type (Authorized, Declined, Emergency), date range, or student name to quickly locate a specific incident or generate a closing-day summary
  • Export to PDF / CSV — One-click export for Ministry of Education compliance submissions, internal school records, or investigation documentation — directly supporting the post-Endarasha regulatory environment described in Chapter 1
  • Summary statistics — Shows students released vs total, average gate processing time (~68 seconds vs the 18–25 minutes observed in the field study), and a Ministry compliance report generator
  • Tamper-proof storage — Records are written to D4: Audit Logs (as shown in the Level 1 DFD) and cannot be edited after the fact; disputes show the original authorization chain including parent confirmation timestamp
Addresses UC-05 & Ministry alignment: Supports the Phase 5 recommendation to integrate SafePass audit logs with the national school safety compliance framework established following the 2024 boarding school safety audit that found 348 schools non-compliant.

Key Design Decisions
No passwords — OTP-only login mirrors M-Pesa familiarity (Jakob's Law). No new cognitive load.
Swahili first — All notifications and USSD menus in Swahili (primary) with English toggle. Not a secondary option.
Offline-capable gate — Teacher's QR scan works without live internet. Critical for rural school gates with intermittent connectivity.
Large touch targets — Confirm Release button is full-width. Designed for elderly users and high-pressure closing day environments.
